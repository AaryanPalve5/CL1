Title: Write a short report on planning, securing, and governing the enterprise architecture.
Aim:

To understand how to plan, secure, and govern enterprise architecture in an organization to improve performance, security, and alignment with business goals.

Theory Summary:

Enterprise Architecture (EA) integrates business strategy with IT infrastructure.

It ensures that IT supports business processes efficiently and securely.

Key Components:

Planning EA

Define business goals and align architecture with them.

Identify stakeholders and create a roadmap.

Establish architecture principles and documentation.

Securing EA

Identify vulnerabilities and apply risk management.

Use encryption, authentication, and access control.

Maintain compliance with security standards.

Governing EA

Set governance policies and roles.

Create review boards and maintain documentation.

Monitor and enforce architectural standards.

Conclusion:
Planning, securing, and governing EA ensures alignment between IT and business, improves agility, and enhances security and compliance.

Title: Design and implement enterprise architecture using TOGAF for banking/healthcare domain.
Aim:

To design an EA framework using TOGAF (The Open Group Architecture Framework) for real-world domains like banking or healthcare.

Theory Summary:

TOGAF provides a step-by-step method (ADM cycle) for building enterprise architectures that align IT with business needs.

TOGAF Phases:

Preliminary Phase: Define scope, goals, and stakeholders.

Architecture Vision: High-level vision aligning IT and business.

Business Architecture: Define business processes (e.g., account management, patient care).

Information System Architecture: Manage data and applications securely.

Technology Architecture: Choose hardware, cloud, and network infrastructure.

Implementation & Migration Planning: Create timelines, budgets, and plans.

Governance: Define compliance and monitoring policies.

Change Management: Handle continuous improvements.

Use Cases:

Banking: Risk management, digital banking integration, regulatory compliance.

Healthcare: Secure EHR, interoperability, HIPAA compliance.

Conclusion:
Using TOGAF ensures that EA is structured, scalable, and compliant, enhancing efficiency in critical sectors like banking and healthcare.
